SLEEP AND REST GUIDE

1. Why Sleep is Important
Sleep is essential for physical and mental health. During sleep, your body repairs itself, consolidates memories, regulates hormones, and processes emotions. Poor sleep increases risk of depression, anxiety, and other health problems.

2. Sleep Recommendations
• Adults: 7-9 hours per night
• Teenagers: 8-10 hours per night
• Children: 9-12 hours per night
• Older adults: 7-8 hours per night

3. Signs of Sleep Problems
• Difficulty falling asleep
• Frequent nighttime awakenings
• Waking up too early
• Daytime sleepiness
• Difficulty concentrating
• Irritability
• Relationship problems due to sleep issues

4. Common Sleep Disorders
• Insomnia: Difficulty falling or staying asleep
• Sleep Apnea: Interrupted breathing during sleep
• Restless Leg Syndrome: Uncomfortable leg sensations
• Narcolepsy: Extreme daytime sleepiness
• Circadian Rhythm Disorders: Sleep schedule misalignment

5. Sleep Hygiene Tips (Healthy Sleep Habits)
• Keep consistent sleep schedule (same bedtime/wake time)
• Make bedroom dark, quiet, and cool (60-67°F)
• Use bed only for sleep and intimacy
• Remove electronic devices from bedroom
• Avoid caffeine after 2 PM
• No alcohol before bed
• Avoid large meals 2-3 hours before sleep
• Exercise daily (but not close to bedtime)
• Use white noise if helpful
• Wash hands and face with warm water before bed

6. Bedtime Routine (30-60 minutes before sleep)
• Dim lights to signal your body it's time to sleep
• Take a warm bath or shower
• Practice relaxation exercises
• Read a book (not on screen)
• Listen to calming music or meditation
• Do light stretching or yoga
• Journal worries or tomorrow's plans
• Try herbal tea (chamomile, lavender)

7. Natural Sleep Aids
• Melatonin: Helps regulate sleep-wake cycle
• Valerian Root: Herbal sleep supplement
• Magnesium: Promotes relaxation
• Lavender: Scent or tea for calming
• Chamomile Tea: Traditional sleep aid
• Warm Milk: Contains tryptophan

8. What to Avoid
• Screens 1 hour before bed (blue light disrupts sleep)
• Caffeine after 2 PM
• Alcohol (may help fall asleep but disrupts sleep quality)
• Large meals close to bedtime
• Naps longer than 30 minutes
• Stressful conversations before bed
• Exercising close to bedtime
• Clock-watching if you can't sleep

9. If You Can't Sleep
• Don't lie in bed awake for more than 20 minutes
• Get up and do a quiet, relaxing activity
• Return to bed only when sleepy
• Don't watch the clock
• Try relaxation techniques
• Avoid checking your phone

10. When to Seek Professional Help
Contact a doctor if:
• Sleep problems persist despite good sleep hygiene
• You snore loudly or stop breathing during sleep
• Daytime sleepiness affects your work or safety
• Sleep problems affect your mood or relationships
• You experience regular nightmares

Remember: Good sleep is an investment in your health and wellbeing!
