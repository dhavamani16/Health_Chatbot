ANXIETY MANAGEMENT GUIDE

1. What is Anxiety?
Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress or fear. While some anxiety is normal, excessive anxiety can interfere with daily life. Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health condition, but they are highly treatable.

2. Types of Anxiety Disorders
• Generalized Anxiety Disorder: Excessive worry about everyday things
• Social Anxiety Disorder: Fear of social situations
• Panic Disorder: Sudden, intense panic attacks
• Specific Phobias: Extreme fear of specific objects or situations
• Separation Anxiety: Fear of being separated from loved ones

3. Physical Symptoms of Anxiety
• Rapid heartbeat or chest pain
• Shortness of breath or hyperventilation
• Dizziness or lightheadedness
• Trembling or shaking
• Sweating
• Nausea or stomach upset
• Muscle tension
• Headaches
• Difficulty sleeping

4. Mental and Emotional Symptoms
• Excessive worrying
• Feeling restless or on edge
• Difficulty concentrating
• Irritability
• Sense of impending doom
• Fear of losing control
• Racing thoughts

5. Common Anxiety Triggers
• Work or school pressure
• Social situations
• Health concerns
• Financial stress
• Major life changes
• Conflicts in relationships
• Caffeine and lack of sleep
• News and social media

6. Quick Anxiety Relief Techniques
• 4-7-8 Breathing: Breathe in for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8
• Grounding Technique (5-4-3-2-1): Notice 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste
• Progressive Muscle Relaxation: Tense and release each muscle group
• Cold Water on Face: Splash cold water or hold ice to reset nervous system
• Move Your Body: Walk, stretch, or do light exercise
• Mindfulness Meditation: Focus on the present moment
• Positive Self-Talk: Remind yourself this feeling will pass

7. Long-Term Management Strategies
• Regular exercise (30 minutes, 5 days/week)
• Maintain consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hours)
• Limit caffeine and alcohol
• Practice yoga or tai chi
• Join support groups
• Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
• Medication if recommended by doctor
• Reduce screen time before bed
• Build a strong support network

8. Prevention Tips
• Identify and avoid triggers when possible
• Develop a daily relaxation routine
• Maintain healthy lifestyle habits
• Build healthy relationships
• Practice saying no to avoid overcommitment
• Regular check-ins with mental health professional

9. When to Seek Help
Contact a healthcare provider if:
• Anxiety interferes with daily activities
• Anxiety lasts more than 2 weeks
• You avoid situations due to anxiety
• Anxiety causes relationship problems
• You have thoughts of self-harm

Remember: Anxiety is manageable, and treatment is effective!
