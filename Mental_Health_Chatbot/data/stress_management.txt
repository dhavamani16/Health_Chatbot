STRESS MANAGEMENT GUIDE

1. What is Stress?
Stress is the body’s natural reaction to challenges or pressure. A small amount of stress can motivate you, but too much stress can affect your mental and physical health.

2. Common Signs of Stress
• Feeling overwhelmed  
• Irritability or mood swings  
• Trouble sleeping  
• Headaches or body pain  
• Difficulty concentrating  
• Increased heart rate  

3. Causes of Stress
• Academic or work pressure  
• Family or relationship issues  
• Financial problems  
• Health concerns  
• Social expectations  
• Major life changes  

4. Healthy Ways to Manage Stress
• Take slow, deep breaths  
• Practice meditation or mindfulness  
• Go for a walk or do light exercise  
• Listen to calming music  
• Talk to someone you trust  
• Take regular breaks from screens  
• Write down your thoughts or feelings  

5. Stress-Relief Activities
• Yoga or stretching  
• Reading a book  
• Drawing or coloring  
• Drinking warm water/tea  
• Spending time in nature  
• Playing with pets  
• Listening to positive podcasts  

6. When Stress Becomes Dangerous
Seek help if you notice:
• Persistent sadness  
• Constant anxiety  
• Loss of interest in activities  
• Isolation from others  
• Thinking about self-harm  

7. Tips for Long-Term Stress Control
• Maintain a healthy routine  
• Sleep 7–9 hours daily  
• Eat balanced meals  
• Limit caffeine and sugar  
• Stay connected with supportive people  
• Set realistic goals  
• Avoid procrastination  

8. Emergency Help
If stress becomes unbearable:
• Talk to a counselor or mental health professional  
• Call a local helpline  
• Reach out to friends or family immediately  

YOU ARE NOT ALONE.  
Stress is temporary — you can overcome it with the right support.
